Ingredients

For the Outer Covering:
  • Rice Flour: 1 cup
  • Water: 1 cup (or as needed)
  • Salt: A pinch
  • Ghee: 1 teaspoon (for greasing)
For the Filling:
  • Grated Coconut: 1 cup (fresh or desiccated)
  • Jaggery: 3/4 cup, grated (adjust to taste)
  • Cardamom Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Chopped Nuts: Optional (like cashews or almonds)

Instructions

Make the Filling:

Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee in a pan on low heat. Add 1 cup of grated coconut and cook for a couple of minutes. Mix in 3/4 cup of grated jaggery and cook until it melts and combines with the coconut. Add 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom powder and mix well. Let it cool.

Prepare the Outer Dough:

In a saucepan, boil 1 cup of water with a pinch of salt. Slowly add 1 cup of rice flour while stirring to avoid lumps. Cook until the mixture thickens and forms a dough. Let it cool slightly, then knead it until smooth.

Shape the Modaks:

Grease your hands with a little ghee. Take a small piece of dough and flatten it into a disc. Place a spoonful of the coconut filling in the center. Fold the edges over the filling to form a cone or use a modak mold.

Steam the Modaks:

Arrange the shaped modaks in a steamer lined with banana leaves or parchment paper. Steam for about 10-15 minutes until shiny and firm.

Serve:

Serve the modaks warm, optionally with a little ghee on top.
